WARTHOG

Their scientific name is Phacochoerus Africanus also known as the common warthog. Their name comes from their ‘warts’ or protrusions on the sides of their face, these protrusions are a combination of bone and cartilage. It protects their face when they fight.

They sleep underground at night in burrows that they steal from other animals such as aardvark. They don’t dig their own.

Warthogs mainly eat grass or will dig for roots and bulbs when it’s dry. If they have the opportunity, they will scavenge on meat as they are omnivorous.

WHERE WILL YOU FIND THEM

North West of the Main entrance next to the Amphitheatre
